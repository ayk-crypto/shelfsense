Additional requirement for Smart Receiving Match Engine:

Supplier invoices may show tax as a collective tax amount for the full line quantity, not as unit tax.

Example invoice columns:

* Item Name
* Qty
* Unit Price
* Total Price
* Tax
* Sum / Final Total

In this format:

* Unit Price is usually price excluding tax
* Total Price is Qty × Unit Price excluding tax
* Tax is the total tax amount for the full line
* Sum is Total Price + Tax

The system must calculate unit tax and unit cost including tax automatically.

Required calculations:
unitTax = lineTaxTotal / quantity
unitCostInclTax = lineTotalInclTax / quantity

Alternative:
unitCostInclTax = unitCostExclTax + unitTax

Example:
Qty = 10
Unit Price Excl. Tax = 100
Total Price Excl. Tax = 1,000
Line Tax Total = 180
Line Total Incl. Tax = 1,180

System should calculate:
Unit Tax = 180 / 10 = 18
Unit Cost Incl. Tax = 1,180 / 10 = 118

Receiving table should support and display:

* Unit Cost Excl. Tax
* Line Subtotal Excl. Tax
* Line Tax Total
* Unit Tax
* Unit Cost Incl. Tax
* Line Total Incl. Tax

Invoice tax format support:

1. Tax per unit
2. Tax per line item
3. Tax only at invoice total level
4. Price already includes tax
5. No tax

If tax is given at line level:

* Divide line tax total by quantity to calculate unit tax.
* Divide final line total including tax by quantity to calculate unit cost including tax.

If tax is only given at invoice total level:

* Allocate tax proportionally across line items based on each line subtotal.
* Mark this as “allocated tax” so user can review.

If price is already tax-inclusive:

* Store extracted unit price as unit cost including tax.
* If tax rate is known, back-calculate tax using:
  unitCostExclTax = unitCostInclTax / (1 + taxRate)
  unitTax = unitCostInclTax - unitCostExclTax

Important:
The receiving screen should not only store one generic unit cost. It should normalize cost into:

* unitCostExclTax
* unitTax
* unitCostInclTax
* lineSubtotalExclTax
* lineTaxTotal
* lineTotalInclTax

For inventory valuation/costing, use unitCostInclTax as the default actual landed unit cost, unless a workspace/accounting setting says to value inventory excluding tax.

Add a workspace-level setting:
Inventory Cost Basis:

* Including Tax
* Excluding Tax

Default should be Including Tax for restaurant use cases.

All calculations should be visible to the user before confirming receiving.
User must be able to override extracted or calculated values before GRN confirmation.
